Deep Dive into Tea Heritage

The tea plantations of Cameron Highlands are more than just a view. They are a 100-year-old tradition started by J.A. Russell in 1929.

Essential Facts:

  • The Species: Every leaf comes from the Camellia sinensis plant.
  • The Altitude: Sitting at 1,500m above sea level, the cool air allows for slower growth and richer flavor.
  • Pruning: The flat top of the bushes, known as the "plucking plateau," is kept at 1 meter height for easier harvesting.

Most of the tea produced here is Black Tea, which undergoes a full oxidation process to develop its signature brisk aroma.
